Delaware's History

Delaware, often referred to as the "First State," holds a unique place in American history. It was the first state to ratify the United States Constitution on December 7, 1787, earning its nickname. Delaware's history is intertwined with the colonial period, as it was one of the original thirteen colonies. The state's history is also marked by its position as a center for commerce and industry, particularly in shipbuilding, manufacturing, and agriculture. Throughout its history, Delaware has been known for its emphasis on individual rights and governance, which contributed to its decision to be a first adopter of the U.S. Constitution. Delaware's rich history is reflected in its many historic sites, museums, and landmarks, making it a fascinating destination for those interested in early American history.

Landscape

Delaware is well known for their beautiful landscapes and beaches. People from up and down the east coast will visit Delaware in the summer to enjoy the nice weather, beautiful beaches, and great scenic views. As Delaware has miles of coastline along the Atlantic Ocean and the Delaware Bay, there are so many places to rest and relax. Delaware's coastline offers a variety of recreational activities, including swimming, sunbathing, and water sports such as surfing and boating. No matter what someone is looking for, they are bound to find it in Delaware.

Fun Facts:

Tax-Free Shopping

Delaware is known for not having a state sales tax, making it a popular shopping destination for visitors looking to save money on retail purchases.

Dover International Speedway

Dover is home to the Dover International Speedway, known as the "Monster Mile," which hosts NASCAR races.

Delaware Parks

Delaware offers a large variety of state parks, despite it being the second smallest State in the US. With 17 parks available, there are many outdoor activities available for everyone to enjoy.

Home of the President

President Joseph R. Biden lived in Delaware prior to becoming the 46th president of the United States. In Delaware, he also served as a state Senator.
